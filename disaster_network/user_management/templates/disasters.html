Understanding Disasters and How to Cope

Natural and man-made disasters can strike at any time. Knowing how to respond and what actions to take during a disaster is crucial to survival and recovery. Below is a guide on different types of disasters and essential survival strategies.

Types of Disasters

Earthquake

Earthquakes can cause immense damage to infrastructure and pose a threat to human life. If indoors, "Drop, Cover, and Hold On." Seek shelter under sturdy furniture and stay away from windows.

Flood

Floods can occur quickly, especially in low-lying areas. Move to higher ground and avoid walking or driving through floodwaters, as they can be deeper and faster than they appear.

Wildfire

Wildfires spread rapidly, especially in dry conditions. Evacuate early if advised, and if trapped, find a body of water or cover yourself in wet cloth to reduce exposure to flames.

What to Do During a Disaster

In any disaster, your first priority should be your safety and the safety of those around you. Here are some general tips for staying safe during different types of disasters:

  • Stay informed by listening to local news or emergency alerts.
  • Follow evacuation orders immediately if given by authorities.
  • Check on neighbors, especially the elderly or those with disabilities.
  • Do not return to dangerous areas until it is declared safe by officials.
